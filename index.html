Resume: Elsje Pieterse (Phd) (PrSciNat)

Summary


I started my career as an agricultural researcher. My research led me to weird and wonderful places. I did research on free ranging Impala, assisted with grazing studies on elephants, did carnivore censuses and game counts on foot, by vehicle or helicopter. I acted as tourist guide, participated in field assessments and game capture and relocation. These activities were not well defined and required a lot of problem identification, ideation, prototyping, testing and starting over. Most of all it required team work!

After this exciting roller coaster and somewhat daunting part off my life I got married and settled into research. My research focus was on increasing efficiency of production, increasing welfare standards and ultimately finding alternatives to the everyday products and system that we used. These alternatives were sought for anti-microbial growth promotors, energy sources and protein sources.

The most educational part of my life was during consultation, since I was unbiased clients trusted me with lots of info and, since I could not share this with anybody else, I ended up with a vast amount of knowledge gained from other people's experience. During this time we changed industries and increased the profitability of intensive farming enterprises.

I was very fortunate to lecture at many Universities and Technicons. I presented short courses to industry and presented lectures at many congresses, conferences and symposia, both locally and abroad. This showed me that what I deem to be common could be very valuable to other people and that many people thirst for this knowledge. I did not have the skill set to develop this and only learned how to create value from this knowledge for both clients and myself.

I believe in our youth and that they are the key to positive change. I lecture to young adults and not only do I teach them subject matter but I also teach them to be fair, and honest. I teach them to think and to maintain a strong ethical code. I also invest in the little ones and present courses on nature, leadership and entrepreneurship to school going children.

I believe that a person should never stop learning and my latest endeavours have resulted in me learning a bit of code, doing website development, online store development and online training courses. This added a lot of value to my life. On the one hand it reduced my dependency on my employer while on the other hand it empowered me to add value to lives of people who desperately needed help in these fields but do not have the means to access this.

My moto in life is simple - if you can imagine it then you can do it. This stems from the wide range of experiences that I was so blessed to have during my life, preparing me in a way which makes me excellent at using a multidisciplinary approach to problem identification and problem solving using innovative means. Failure is just never an option and difficulties only lead to greater success.
